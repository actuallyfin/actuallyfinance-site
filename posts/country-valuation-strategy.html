Country Valuation Strategy: Findings & Robustness

A web version of the report covering a rules-based country rotation strategy using MSCI IMI net-return data, Barclays/Faber CAPE valuations, trend filters, momentum exclusions, and an EIU Democracy Index filter.

Executive Summary

The locked-in baseline ranks 44 MSCI IMI countries by CAPE, selects the cheapest septile, applies a 200-day SMA trend filter, excludes countries that have freshly turned negative on momentum, and removes countries with a declining illiberal Democracy Index profile.

In the report window from 1997-10-07 to 2026-04-16, the strategy produced a 13.9% CAGR and 0.91 Sharpe after 10 bps per side transaction costs, compared with 8.2% for an equal-weight basket of the same country universe.

What The Strategy Does

  • CAPE selection: rank countries from cheapest to most expensive and hold the cheapest septile.
  • Trend overlay: hold selected countries only when price is above the 200-day moving average.
  • Fresh-negative momentum: exclude countries whose trailing return has turned negative after a previously positive window.
  • Democracy filter: exclude countries below a Democracy Index threshold when the score is also deteriorating.

Robustness Notes

The report emphasizes that the outperformance is broad-based across subperiods, and a leave-one-country-out test suggests no single country is load-bearing. It also flags real limitations: Democracy Index data starts in 2006, several Eastern European CAPE histories begin later than the full sample, and parameter choices are partly informed by the same data used to measure performance.
