Recession Timing Dashboard: Signals, Regimes, and Rules

A placeholder research note for the dashboard that tracks recession stress, economic-cycle signals, and market-timing context for an investing strategy.

What This Piece Will Cover

The article can explain the dashboard's signal framework: which indicators are leading, which are coincident, how each signal turns cautionary or recessionary, and how the composite score should be interpreted.

The goal is not to predict every recession date exactly. The useful framing is whether macro conditions are normal, deteriorating, or already stressed enough to justify a different portfolio posture.

Draft note: This placeholder can become the methodology page for the dashboard, including thresholds, indicator definitions, update cadence, and historical false positives.

Possible Strategy Rules

  • Expansion: risk assets stay fully engaged when most leading and financial-condition indicators are healthy.
  • Caution: reduce cyclical exposure or raise quality when multiple leading indicators cross warning thresholds.
  • Recessionary: shift toward capital preservation when labor, credit, and growth signals confirm broad stress.

Open Questions

The article should also be explicit about limitations: revisions to macro data, lagged releases, threshold overfitting, and the difference between recession detection and market timing.
